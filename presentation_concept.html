Presentation Concept: Tool Demo

The Meteorite Illusion: Exposing Human Bias in the Search for Cosmic Treasure.

Format: Interactive visual demo (no slides). This document provides the required briefing in bullet-point form, as permitted for tool demos.  ·  Course: PODSV FS26

1 Topic & Main Goal

Topic: A socio-economic, geographic and economic analysis of meteorite discoveries worldwide. NASA records where meteorites were found, but this data is heavily biased by human presence and environmental conditions.

Main goal

  • Deconstruct the “Discovery Gap”: show that meteorite-finding maps are largely maps of human activity and geography.
  • Correlate NASA finds with population density (human presence), aridity (preservation potential) and GDP per capita (economic context).
  • Integrate a market-value estimation based on meteorite classification (recclass) and mass: a “Fair Value” benchmark.
  • Optional milestone:Shift from a map of findings to an interactive Opportunity & Value Map that protects collectors from fraud and helps explorers locate high-potential, unrecorded search zones.

2 Target Group

  • Meteorite collectors & investors (Persona “Lukas”, amateur investor): need independent price benchmarks to avoid overpaying for scam or fake listings.
  • Expedition planners / field hunters (Persona “Sarah”, professional geologist): seek under-searched, high-preservation zones with low human search history.
  • Secondary: lecturers evaluating analytical depth and the handling of data bias; the project team itself.
Primary audience for the demo: collectors/investors and field hunters: people who need to turn a biased “finds” map into actionable opportunity and value insight.

3 Narrative Elements & How They Are Expressed

The product is a guided data story with light exploration, structured as a three-act arc:

  • The Illusion: NASA’s raw finds map, which appears to show where meteorites “are”.
  • The Explanation: overlay human and environmental filters (population density, GDP, aridity) to expose the bias.
  • The Revelation(optional): the Opportunity Map plus the estimated financial worth of undiscovered falls.

Expressed by means of…

  • Visualisations: interactive Plotly world map: country-level choropleth (aridity, population density, GDP) plus a pixel-level aridity heatmap; metric switcher to move between narrative acts.
  • Media: web-based Quarto application published on GitHub Pages; an interactive demo instead of static slides.
  • Dramatization: the deliberate “illusion → reality” reveal; toggling layers visibly dismantles the naïve reading of the raw map.
  • Rhetorics: framing finds as a proxy for human activity, not meteorite abundance; the “Fair Value” tooltip as a trust/anti-scam device.
  • Annotation: short explanatory text per view to reduce cognitive load and guide interpretation.

4 Aspects We Expect Feedback On

  • Does the “Discovery Gap” narrative (illusion → explanation → revelation) come across clearly and convincingly?
  • Is the Estimated Market Value model (median price-per-gram per recclass) credible and useful as a benchmark, given the limited available price dataset?
  • Is the data-bias message (finds ≈ human presence) communicated effectively to a non-expert audience?

5 Presentation Concept (Tool Demo, no slides)

  1. Intro: state topic, main goal and target group.
  2. The Illusion: open the raw NASA distribution / aridity heatmap and let it “mislead”.
  3. The Explanation: switch metrics (population density → GDP → aridity) to expose the human/geographic bias.
  4. The Revelation: use hover tooltips for per-country detail and the “Fair Value” estimate; point out high-potential under-searched zones.
  5. Wrap & feedback: restate the Discovery-Gap takeaway and ask the audience for feedback on the points in section 4.
